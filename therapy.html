Therapy Services

Finding your way forward

Whether you're seeking support for yourself, your relationship, or your family, I offer a warm, collaborative space to explore what's possible.

Who I work with

I work with adults of all ages who are looking for a thoughtful, non-judgmental space to explore life's challenges. Whether you're navigating a specific situation or seeking deeper self-understanding, therapy can help you reconnect with your values and find your own path forward.

I particularly enjoy working with people who feel ready to look at their lives with fresh eyes. People who sense that the stories they've been telling themselves may not be serving them anymore. People who are curious about what else might be possible.

You don't need to have it all figured out. You don't need to know exactly what you want to work on. Many people come to therapy with a vague sense that something needs to shift, and that's enough.

Individual Therapy

Individual therapy is a space for you to slow down and be heard. Many people come to me when they're feeling stuck, overwhelmed, or uncertain about who they are or what they want. Some are dealing with specific challenges like anxiety or depression. Others are navigating major life transitions or simply seeking more clarity and purpose.

Common themes I work with:

Anxiety and worry
Depression and low mood
Life transitions
Career uncertainty
Identity questions
Burnout and exhaustion
Self-doubt and inner criticism
Grief and loss
Relationship patterns
Perfectionism
Stress management
Building self-compassion

What we'll do together

In individual therapy, we'll work to understand the stories that have shaped your experience, both the ones that serve you and the ones that don't. We'll explore your values, your strengths, and the moments when you've felt most like yourself.

Rather than focusing on what's "wrong" with you, we'll look at how problems have affected your life and what you'd prefer instead. You'll gain insight, but more importantly, you'll reconnect with your own sense of agency and direction.

Sessions are typically 50 minutes, and we meet weekly, though frequency can be adjusted as needed.

Couples Therapy

Every relationship has its own story, and sometimes those stories get complicated. Couples therapy creates a space where both partners can be heard, understood, and supported in finding new ways to connect.

People come to couples therapy for many reasons. Some are in crisis; others simply want to strengthen what they already have. There's no wrong time to seek support for your relationship.

Common reasons couples seek therapy:

Communication breakdowns
Recurring conflicts
Feeling disconnected
Trust issues
Major life decisions
Parenting disagreements
Intimacy concerns
Blending families
Navigating transitions
Processing past hurts

What we'll do together

In couples therapy, I work with both partners to understand the patterns that have developed in your relationship. We'll look at how you communicate, what gets triggered for each of you, and the stories you've created together about who you are as a couple.

My approach is balanced and curious. I'm not here to take sides or assign blame. Instead, we'll work together to understand how you've both contributed to the current situation, and more importantly, how you can both contribute to something different.

Sessions are typically 75-90 minutes, and we usually meet weekly or every other week.

Family Therapy

Families are complex systems with their own histories, patterns, and ways of relating. When one person is struggling, the whole family often feels it. Family therapy addresses these interconnected dynamics, helping everyone in the family find new ways to support each other.

Family therapy isn't about identifying a "problem person." It's about understanding how the family works as a whole and creating space for everyone to be seen and heard.

Common reasons families seek therapy:

Parent-child conflict
Adolescent challenges
Sibling relationships
Divorce and separation
Blended family issues
Communication problems
Generational patterns
Major life changes
Grief and loss
Supporting a family member

What we'll do together

In family therapy, we'll explore the patterns and dynamics that shape how your family operates. We'll look at the stories each person holds about the family and about their role within it.

I create a space where every family member can speak and be heard, regardless of age. We'll work on communication, understanding, and finding new ways to support each other through whatever challenges you're facing.

Sessions are typically 60-90 minutes. Not every family member needs to attend every session, and we can adjust the format to fit your family's needs.

My Approach

What is narrative therapy?

Narrative therapy is built on a simple but powerful idea: we make sense of our lives through stories. The stories we tell about ourselves, often shaped by our families, cultures, and experiences, profoundly influence how we see ourselves and what we believe is possible.

Sometimes the stories we carry are limiting. We might think of ourselves as "the anxious one" or believe we're "bad at relationships." These stories can feel like facts, but they're actually just one interpretation among many.

In narrative therapy, we work together to:

  • - Externalize problems (separate "you" from "the problem")
  • - Discover "unique outcomes" or exceptions to the dominant story
  • - Explore your values and what truly matters to you
  • - Build and thicken your "preferred story"
  • - Recognize your own agency and authorship over your life

This approach is non-pathologizing, collaborative, and deeply respectful of your lived experience. You're not broken. You don't need to be fixed. You just need space to rediscover what you already know.
