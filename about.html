About Me

Hello, I'm Elizabeth

I'm a licensed marriage and family therapist who believes that you already have what you need to live the life you want. My job is to help you find it.

Why I became a therapist

I've always been fascinated by stories: the ones we inherit from our families, the ones we tell ourselves, and the ones we're still writing. I came to therapy because I wanted to help people discover that they have more authorship over their lives than they might realize.

Before becoming a therapist, I spent years in roles where I learned to listen deeply and hold space for complexity. That experience taught me that people don't need to be fixed. They need to be heard, respected, and gently invited to see themselves and their situations from new angles.

I believe therapy should feel like a partnership. You bring your expertise about your own life; I bring curiosity, warmth, and a set of tools we can explore together. There's no script, no judgment, and no pretending to have all the answers. Just two people working together to understand what's happening and what's possible.

"I don't see my role as telling you who to be. I see it as helping you reconnect with who you already are, and supporting you as you step more fully into that."

Working Together

What to expect in sessions

When we work together, you can expect a space that's warm, genuine, and free of judgment. I won't rush you or push you anywhere you're not ready to go. At the same time, I won't let you stay stuck if we can help it.

Sessions are typically conversational. We might explore a specific situation that's been on your mind, trace patterns that keep showing up in your life, or slow down to examine what certain experiences have meant to you. Sometimes we'll work with specific techniques; other times we'll simply talk and reflect.

I tend to ask a lot of questions, not because I'm testing you, but because I'm genuinely curious. I want to understand what matters to you, what you've been through, and what kind of life you're hoping to build. Your answers guide where we go.

Above all, you can expect to be treated as a whole person, not a set of symptoms. I'm interested in your strengths, your values, your moments of joy, not just your struggles. We'll work with all of it.

My Therapeutic Style

How I work

Collaborative

We're partners in this work. I bring my training and perspective; you bring your lived experience and wisdom about your own life.

Reflective

I help you slow down and look at things from different angles. Sometimes the most powerful work happens when we pause to notice.

Interactive

Therapy isn't passive. I'll ask questions, offer observations, and engage actively in our conversation. We think together.

What I draw from

My work is grounded in narrative therapy, which focuses on the stories we tell about ourselves and our lives, and how those stories shape our experience. I also draw from collaborative therapy, systemic approaches, and attachment theory. I believe in meeting each person where they are and adapting my approach to what's most helpful for you.
