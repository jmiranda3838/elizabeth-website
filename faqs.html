Getting Started

The first step is scheduling a free 15-minute consultation. This gives us a chance to talk briefly about what brings you to therapy and whether we might be a good fit. You can request a consultation through my contact page or by calling (949) 444-3899.

If we decide to move forward, we'll find a time for your first full session. I typically have availability within 1-2 weeks.

Our first session is about getting to know each other. I'll ask questions about what brings you to therapy, your history, and what you're hoping to get out of our work together. You'll also have space to ask me anything you'd like.

There's no pressure to share everything at once. We'll move at a pace that feels comfortable for you. By the end of the session, we'll have a sense of direction and can discuss how often we might meet.

The relationship between therapist and client is one of the most important factors in successful therapy. That's why I offer a free consultation, so you can get a sense of me before committing.

Pay attention to how you feel during our conversation. Do you feel heard? Comfortable? Like you could open up to me? It's okay if you're nervous (that's normal), but you should feel a basic sense of connection and trust.

If after a few sessions you feel we're not the right fit, I'm happy to help you find another therapist who might be better suited to your needs.

Logistics

Individual therapy sessions are 50 minutes. Couples sessions are typically 75-90 minutes, and family sessions range from 60-90 minutes depending on the needs of the family.

Most clients meet weekly, especially at the beginning of therapy. This frequency helps build momentum and allows us to work more deeply. As therapy progresses and you're feeling more stable, we may shift to every other week or monthly sessions.

Ultimately, the frequency is something we'll decide together based on your needs, goals, and circumstances.

Yes! I offer secure video sessions for clients anywhere in California. Telehealth makes therapy more accessible and fits into busy lives. Many clients find it just as effective as in-person work.

I use a HIPAA-compliant platform to ensure your privacy. All you need is a private space, a reliable internet connection, and a device with a camera.

I ask for at least 24 hours' notice if you need to cancel or reschedule. Late cancellations or missed sessions are charged the full session fee.

I understand that emergencies happen. If something truly unexpected comes up, please reach out and we can discuss.

Fees & Insurance

Individual therapy sessions (50 min): $175

Couples therapy sessions (75-90 min): $225

Family therapy sessions (60-90 min): $200

I offer a limited number of sliding scale spots for those who need them. If cost is a barrier, please mention it during our consultation and we can discuss options.

I am an out-of-network provider, which means I don't bill insurance directly. However, if you have out-of-network benefits, you may be able to receive partial reimbursement for our sessions.

I can provide you with a superbill (a detailed receipt) that you can submit to your insurance company for potential reimbursement. I recommend contacting your insurance provider to ask about your out-of-network mental health benefits before we begin.

I accept credit cards, debit cards, and HSA/FSA cards. Payment is due at the time of the session.

Under the No Surprises Act, you have the right to receive a "Good Faith Estimate" explaining how much your care will cost. This estimate is based on the information we have at the time and may change as treatment evolves.

I will provide you with a Good Faith Estimate before we begin therapy. If you have questions about this, please don't hesitate to ask.

About Therapy

Narrative therapy is based on the idea that we make sense of our lives through stories. The stories we tell about ourselves, often shaped by our families, culture, and experiences, influence how we see ourselves and what we believe is possible.

In narrative therapy, we work to understand the stories that have been shaping your life, separate you from the problems you're facing (you are not the problem), and discover alternative stories that align with your values and hopes.

It's a respectful, non-pathologizing approach that honors your expertise on your own life.

There's no fixed timeline for therapy. Some people come for a few months to work through a specific issue; others engage in longer-term work to address deeper patterns. It depends on your goals, your situation, and how quickly things shift.

We'll check in regularly about how you're feeling and whether therapy is meeting your needs. You're always in control of when to continue and when to end.

Yes, confidentiality is fundamental to therapy. What you share stays between us, with a few legal exceptions that I'll explain fully before we begin (such as imminent risk of harm to yourself or others, or suspected abuse of a child or vulnerable adult).

For couples and family therapy, we'll discuss confidentiality boundaries at the start so everyone understands how information will be handled.

Important: Crisis & Emergency Information

Therapy is not appropriate for crisis situations. If you are in immediate danger or experiencing a mental health emergency, please take one of the following steps:

Call 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline, available 24/7)
Call 911 or go to your nearest emergency room
Text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line)

Please do not use email or my contact form to communicate urgent matters, as I may not see messages immediately.
